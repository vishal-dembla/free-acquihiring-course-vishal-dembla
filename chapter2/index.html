Chapter 2 — Deal Models: Acquihire-Only, Asset/Share Deals, Team Lift-Outs

Meta Description: Explore the main deal models for acquihiring in India — acquihire-only, asset/share acquisitions, and team lift-outs — with examples, pros/cons, and recruiter insights.

2.1 Why Deal Models Matter

Acquihiring may sound simple—“buy a startup for its people”—but the deal structure decides how smooth or messy the process becomes. Think of it like player transfers in sports. You could buy the whole club, acquire just its training rights, or poach an entire team unit from within. The legal and financial wrapper changes incentives, integration, and risks.

In India, three models dominate: Acquihire-Only, Asset/Share Deals, and Team Lift-Outs. Each has a distinct logic, negotiation dynamic, and compliance burden.

2.2 Acquihire-Only

Definition: An acquihire-only deal transfers the team into the acquirer without buying the startup’s legal entity or IP.

This is the cleanest form of acquihiring. The acquirer offers jobs, signing bonuses, and retention packages to the target team. Founders may get a one-time payout or join in leadership roles. The original startup entity usually winds down after closing.

Caselet: In 2020, Razorpay acquihired a small fintech startup in Bengaluru. Rather than acquire the company’s app (which had little traction), Razorpay folded in the engineers to boost its payments stack.

  • Pros: Fast, light on compliance, minimal liabilities.
  • Cons: Employees may see it as a “soft landing,” not a growth path. Investors often recover little.

2.3 Asset or Share Deals

Sometimes the acquirer prefers to buy either the assets (like codebase, IP rights, or office leases) or the shares (taking control of the startup entity itself).

Aspect Asset Deal Share Deal
Scope Select assets (tech, IP, office) Entire legal entity
Liabilities Mostly excluded Inherited with company
Speed Medium Slowest (regulatory + diligence)
Best For Tech/IP plus team Scaling with licenses, contracts, or global subs

Caselet: In 2018, Flipkart acquired F1 Info Solutions, not just for its people but also for its service centers. That was structured as a share acquisition to retain ongoing contracts and licenses.

  • Pros: Useful if IP or contracts are valuable.
  • Cons: Slower, heavier diligence, higher legal costs.

2.4 Team Lift-Outs

Definition: A team lift-out is when a company directly hires an entire team (often with founder consent, sometimes quietly), without buying the entity or assets.

Think of it as acquihiring-lite. The acquirer offers roles to 10–30 people who already work as a pod. The original company may survive, pivot, or downsize.

Caselet: Several GCCs in Hyderabad have used lift-outs to spin up AI/ML pods by hiring intact startup teams. No formal acquisition—just bulk offers with retention clauses.

  • Pros: No M&A paperwork, quick, pure talent play.
  • Cons: Can create legal disputes if IP or client contracts are left behind; weaker investor alignment.

2.5 Recruiter’s Lens

Each model shifts how recruiters and HR heads operate:

  • Acquihire-Only: Focus on retention packages and cultural onboarding.
  • Asset/Share Deal: Prepare for compliance mapping (ESOPs, labor dues, contracts).
  • Lift-Outs: Watch for non-compete and IP clauses to avoid litigation risks.

2.6 Risks Across Models

  • Legal Grey Zones: Lift-outs sometimes trigger disputes over IP ownership.
  • Employee Morale: Teams may feel uncertain if deal terms aren’t transparent.
  • Investor Pushback: Asset/share deals without proper valuation can leave investors unhappy.
  • Integration Gaps: Models with less structure (lift-outs) can miss out on onboarding discipline.

2.7 Summary

No deal model is inherently better—it depends on what the acquirer values. Acquihire-only is fastest when pure talent is the goal. Asset/share deals work if IP, contracts, or licenses add value. Team lift-outs are lean but risky if legal and cultural integration is ignored.

For recruiters, the key is understanding the deal wrapper so you can anticipate risks and design retention strategies early.

FAQs

  • Q1. What’s the fastest acquihire model?
    Acquihire-only, since it avoids entity-level compliance.
  • Q2. Do investors benefit in acquihire-only deals?
    Usually very little—value accrues to employees and sometimes founders.
  • Q3. Why use a share deal in India?
    To retain licenses, contracts, or tax structures tied to the legal entity.
  • Q4. Are team lift-outs legal?
    Yes, but acquirers must avoid poaching IP or breaching non-compete clauses.
  • Q5. Which model is most common for GCCs?
    Team lift-outs, since GCCs often need pods without acquiring entities.

Quick Quiz

  1. Which deal model excludes most liabilities?
    a) Share deal
    b) Asset deal
    c) Lift-out
    Answer: b) Asset deal.
  2. True or False: Acquihire-only means the acquirer must buy the startup’s legal entity.
    Answer: False.
  3. Which model is riskiest for IP disputes?
    a) Acquihire-only
    b) Asset deal
    c) Team lift-out
    Answer: c) Team lift-out.
